The Reason for Assembly Language

In the very beginning of computers, they had to be programmed by toggling 1's and 0's into memory to construct the instructions.

For example, say you want to perform the operation of adding the 8bit AC register to the bit value of 37. To do that on an 8086 chip, you would enter these bits:
00100100 00100101
Base 16: 23 25
Base 10: 35 37

The first 7 bits specify the arithmetic operation.
The second 7 bits is the data.

Assembly: AND AC, 37

It all started with someone punching in the bits.
